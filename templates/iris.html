Problem description

Classifying species based on numerical data can be challenging due to the non-obvious relationships between features and their corresponding categories. In the Iris dataset, variables such as sepal and petal measurements make manual classification difficult and inefficient at scale. Therefore, a Linear Discriminant Analysis (LDA) model is proposed to identify patterns in the data and automatically predict the species, with performance evaluated using metrics such as accuracy and the confusion matrix.

Dataset Explanation

The Iris dataset is one of the most widely used datasets in machine learning for classification tasks. It contains measurements of 150 flowers from three different species: Iris-setosa, Iris-versicolor, and Iris-virginica. Each observation includes four numerical features: sepal length, sepal width, petal length, and petal width (in centimeters). These variables enable the analysis of differences between species and provide the foundation for training models that can automatically classify new flowers based on their characteristics.
